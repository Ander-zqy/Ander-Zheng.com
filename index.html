前言

4 月 25–26 日，我们承办了一场百人黑客松——抖音 AI 创变者计划 2026 黑客松联赛·宁波站，这是宁波市第一场市级黑客松。我们用两天时间聚集了百名选手、数百名观众。抖音 AI 创变者计划总负责人亲自给大家开 seminar，我们还请到了浙大系 Delta X 迭代未来孵化器、浙大 x-lab 的两位 co-founder 讲创新创业生态；请到了 3 位清华校友——AA 加速器创始人、启迪之星（杭州）负责人、杭州智舟一跃创始人开 OPC 论坛……

我们看到了不少亮点：

  • 比如一位专升本同学可以做出有趣、优秀乃至伟大的作品；
  • 比如一个队伍 4 名选手可能来自 4 个不同国家；
  • 比如工作人员们对黑客松的举办很积极很上心；
  • 比如嘉宾们从北京、上海、杭州赶来，为了支持年轻人、支持创变者；
  • 比如我们很多不同领域不同阶段认识的朋友参与进来；
  • 比如大厂、孵化器、老师和高校学生可以聊成一片；
  • 比如我们真的以学生身份给政产学研商贡献了一个小小的「新」的东西；
  • 比如我们真的在宁波塑造着创新的、求变的氛围。

但同时也发现了一些值得深思的现象：在 AI 时代，北、上、深、杭的黑客松已经近乎多得要泛滥，科创活动每天在各式各样的社群里饱满，高校学生在新创社区和初创领域普遍活跃。但在东部沿海、经济相对发达、充满浙商创新探索精神的宁波，这还是第一次由年轻人主动引过来一场市级 AI 黑客松。（其中，有太多朋友在这个过程中问我们「什么是黑客松？」）

新的涌现

从大模型、agent，到 vibe coding、mcp、rag、skills、harness engineering、AI native、超级个体、opc……AI 时代名词的迭代飞快，而兴奋、焦虑、迷茫、fomo 等情绪也广泛蔓延。同时，AI 初创变多了，年轻创业者变多了，美元募资回暖，高校学生对新领域好奇心上升，AI 等新创活动越来越多……当新的人、新的钱、新的社区、新的孵化、新的生态、新的内容正在涌现的今天，我们又该如何发生？

从高校开始，自下而上

去年夏天（大一升大二的暑假），我在北京海淀中关村做了一段创投生态相关的实习，调研前沿科技相关的人、项目、投资机构、社区以及高校学生群体。之后我就不断地去参加各种社区的创客节、黑客松等活动，也 connect 到了很多有意思的人。这算是我第一次接触到这些生态。

AI 这一波让我看到了很好的生态。但我也同时发现高校学生离产业界实在是太远了，也离市场、离需求、离用户、离场景太远了。这不是谁的错，而是多种因素共同作用的结果：我们看到，有创造性潜质的学生却缺乏将想象落地的「创造的工具」；我们同时发现，那些已将想象落地、拥有产品能力的同学却链接不到「商业化资源」。

一堂创业教育课、一个偶然的 idea、一份创赛的 BP、一个 vibe coding 出的小玩具、一个在实验室里面的成果……这些对于一个真正可商业化的创业项目来说，可能是 1、可能是 0、更可能是 −1。但究其方法论，我们真的需要自下而上的学生群体来推动氛围的搭建。也就是说：让年轻人去做年轻的事。

所以我在去年 10 月回到学校之后就创立了 NEIC 宁诺创投俱乐部，一个在创投与 AI 领域链接高校学生群体与产业界的社区，是宁波第一个、也是国内比较少有的这种从高校出来的独立民间学生群体。我们目前有社员约 800 人，核心社员 180 人，办了 5 场活动，积累了 30 多个合作伙伴。很好的一点是，至少对一部分人来说，社员有时会跟我说我们的社群真实地帮到了他——帮他找到项目早期的方向、去哪里实习的方向、如何获取信息的方向、怎么找到好活动的方向。

拓荒：从 −1 到 0

一个产品从 idea 到正式向公众 launch，一个创业项目拿到第一笔融资……这是伟大的「从 0 到 1」。但在我们深入到高校的创投生态中却发现，能够正确抵达「0」的学生少之又少，大家似乎同时需要的是如何「从 −1 到 0」。

「从 −1 到 0」更像是 NEIC 在做的事：我们通过每天分层的多样化信息发布（AI 前沿、创投报告、技术报告、最新实习 JD 信息整理、科创活动信息等等）、活动举行、项目从 −1 到 0 的扶持，赋能我们的社员；也把初创项目、社区、企业、孵化器和投资机构引进来，一起进行内容与活动的生态合作。或许这件事本身很微小，但至少在半年前，我身边 99% 的文商科专业学生跟我说 AI 离他们太远太远，说创业好像就是在创赛上写个 BP。

未来 · 我们怎样发生？

我们觉得 NEIC 做的事情至少对部分人很有意义，所以我们打算把这种氛围深入渗透到宁波所有高校——launch 首个覆盖全宁波高校的科创社区：Emerge·甬现。对于爱好者，我们推进技术普惠：优质 AI 信息、知识共学、交流论坛、实操型 workshop；对于有成熟想法、方向与团队的 founders、builders，我们通过链接高校、政府、社区、孵化器等资源，促进项目落地。

欢迎宁波高校学生与我们共创。

拓荒：從 −1 到 0

一個產品從 idea 到正式向公眾 launch，一個創業項目拿到第一筆融資……這是偉大的「從 0 到 1」。但在我們深入到高校的創投生態中卻發現，能夠正確抵達「0」的學生少之又少，大家似乎同時需要的是如何「從 −1 到 0」。

「從 −1 到 0」更像是 NEIC 在做的事：我們通過每天分層的多樣化信息發布（AI 前沿、創投報告、技術報告、最新實習 JD 信息整理、科創活動信息等等）、活動舉行、項目從 −1 到 0 的扶持，賦能我們的社員；也把初創項目、社區、企業、孵化器和投資機構引進來，一起進行內容與活動的生態合作。或許這件事本身很微小，但至少在半年前，我身邊 99% 的文商科專業學生跟我說 AI 離他們太遠太遠，說創業好像就是在創賽上寫個 BP。

未來 · 我們怎樣發生？

我們覺得 NEIC 做的事情至少對部分人很有意義，所以我們打算把這種氛圍深入滲透到寧波所有高校——launch 首個覆蓋全寧波高校的科創社區：Emerge·甬現。對於愛好者，我們推進技術普惠：優質 AI 信息、知識共學、交流論壇、實操型 workshop；對於有成熟想法、方向與團隊的 founders、builders，我們通過鏈接高校、政府、社區、孵化器等資源，促進項目落地。

歡迎寧波高校學生與我們共創。

Preface

On April 25–26 we hosted a hundred-person hackathon — the Douyin AI Changemaker Program 2026 Hackathon League, Ningbo Station — the first city-level hackathon Ningbo has ever had. Over two days we brought together a hundred contestants and several hundred spectators. The program's overall lead ran a seminar in person; we also invited the two co-founders of ZJU's Delta X "Iterate the Future" incubator and ZJU x-lab to talk about the innovation-and-startup ecosystem, and three Tsinghua alumni — the founder of AA Accelerator, the head of TusStar (Hangzhou), and the founder of Hangzhou Zhizhou Yiyue — for an OPC forum.

We saw plenty of bright spots:

  • A student on a vocational-to-undergraduate path could build something interesting, excellent, even great;
  • A single team of four might come from four different countries;
  • Our staff threw themselves into making the hackathon happen;
  • Guests rushed in from Beijing, Shanghai, and Hangzhou just to back young people and changemakers;
  • Friends we'd met across many fields and stages all pitched in;
  • Big-tech folks, incubators, professors, and students all talked as one;
  • As students, we genuinely contributed a small "new" thing to the government-industry-academia-research-business world;
  • We're genuinely shaping an atmosphere of innovation and change in Ningbo.

But we also noticed things worth sitting with. In the AI era, hackathons in Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, and Hangzhou are almost overflowing; sci-tech events fill every kind of community daily; students are broadly active in startup circles. Yet in Ningbo — coastal, relatively prosperous, steeped in the Zhejiang-merchant spirit of enterprise — this was the first time young people themselves pulled in a city-level AI hackathon. (Along the way, far too many friends asked us, "What even is a hackathon?")

New emergence

From large models and agents to vibe coding, MCP, RAG, skills, harness engineering, AI-native, super-individuals, OPC… the vocabulary of the AI era turns over fast, and excitement, anxiety, confusion, and FOMO spread just as widely. At the same time, AI startups are multiplying, young founders are multiplying, USD fundraising is warming up, students are more curious about new fields, and AI events keep growing. When new people, new money, new communities, new incubation, new ecosystems, and new content are all emerging — how do we make ourselves happen?

Starting from campus, bottom-up

Last summer (the break between freshman and sophomore year), I did an internship around the venture ecosystem in Zhongguancun, Beijing, researching the people, projects, investors, communities, and student groups in frontier tech. After that I kept going to maker fairs, hackathons, and other community events, and connected with a lot of interesting people. That was my first real contact with this ecosystem.

This wave of AI showed me a great ecosystem. But I also realized college students are simply too far from industry — too far from the market, from real demand, from users, from concrete scenarios. This isn't anyone's fault; it's the product of many forces at once: students with creative potential who lack the "tools to create" that bring imagination to ground, and students who've already shipped real products but can't connect to "commercialization resources."

A startup class, a chance idea, a competition business plan, a toy vibe-coded into being, a result sitting in a lab… for a truly commercializable venture, each might be a 1, a 0, more likely a −1. But methodologically, we really do need bottom-up student communities to drive the building of an atmosphere. In other words: let young people do young things.

So in October last year, after returning to school, I founded NEIC — the UNNC Venture Club — a community linking students with industry across venture and AI; Ningbo's first, and one of the rarer independent, grassroots student groups in the country to come out of a university. We currently have around 800 members, 180 core members, five events run, and over thirty partners. The good part: at least for some, members tell me the community genuinely helped them — find early direction for a project, figure out where to intern, learn which information to seek, and how to find good events.

Pioneering: from −1 to 0

A product going from idea to a public launch; a venture landing its first round — that's the great "0 to 1." But going deep into the campus venture ecosystem, we found very few students even reach "0" correctly; what everyone also needs is how to go "from −1 to 0."

"From −1 to 0" is essentially what NEIC does: through daily layered, varied information (AI frontiers, venture reports, tech reports, curated internship JDs, sci-tech event info), through events, and through −1-to-0 support, we empower our members; we also bring in startups, communities, companies, incubators, and investors to collaborate across the ecosystem on content and events. Maybe it's a small thing, but as recently as half a year ago, 99% of the humanities-and-business students around me told me AI was far, far from them, and that "starting up" basically meant writing a business plan for a competition.

The future — how do we happen?

We think what NEIC does matters, at least to some — so we set out to push this atmosphere deep into every university in Ningbo, launching the first sci-tech community spanning all of them: Emerge·Yongxian. For enthusiasts, we push tech accessibility — high-quality AI information, co-learning, forums, and hands-on workshops; for founders and builders with mature ideas, direction, and teams, we help projects land by linking universities, government, communities, and incubators.

Ningbo university students — come build with us.
